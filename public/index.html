High Temperature

Alloys that maintain superior physical and mechanical integrity at elevated temperatures are crucial in the fastener industry. They are essential for high-temperature applications including furnace and related equipment manufacturing, steam turbine production, metal and mineral refining, metal forming, chemical processing, and power generation.

Low Temperature

Fasteners designed to withstand significant impact while maintaining structural integrity at low temperatures are essential for low-temperature bolting applications. These fasteners are typically manufactured from AISI or SAE 4340 alloy and conform to various ASTM specifications, including A320 L43, L7, L7M, B21, B23, and B24.

Weathering Steel

A key category of bolting alloys is known for its high durability and resistance to corrosion and erosion caused by harsh, year-round weather conditions. These properties extend the product's life cycle and reduce maintenance costs. When exposed to the atmosphere, these alloys form an adherent protective layer of rust or patina, which helps prevent further corrosion.

Marine Grade

Marine-grade bolting is specifically engineered using materials that offer resistance to saltwater corrosion and erosion, while also providing high strength and low-temperature performance. Commonly used materials in this category include stainless steels and copper alloys. Alternatively, high-strength alloy steels with corrosion-resistant coatings are also utilized to endure the demanding conditions of marine environments.

Corrosion Resistant

This category of bolting alloys offers exceptional resistance to various forms of corrosion, including general corrosion, intergranular corrosion, localized corrosion, galvanic corrosion, and stress corrosion cracking under diverse environmental conditions. We supply a wide range of bolts and fasteners made from stainless steel alloys, copper alloys, tantalum, zirconium, pure nickel, and other corrosion-resistant nickel-based alloys.
